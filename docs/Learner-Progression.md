# Learner Progression

## Purpose

This document defines how ARIA gradually transitions the learner from assisted language learning to natural immersion.

The goal is not rapid memorization. The goal is confident communication.

ARIA measures readiness rather than time spent in the application.

---

## Guiding Philosophy

Immersion should never feel overwhelming.

Instead of suddenly removing English support, ARIA gradually increases the learner's exposure to Gaeilge as confidence and comprehension improve.

Support should disappear naturally—and return immediately whenever it is needed.

The learner should always feel challenged, but never lost.

---

## Learning Priorities

ARIA prioritizes learning in the following order:

1. Confidence
2. Comprehension
3. Communication
4. Pronunciation
5. Accuracy
6. Grammar

Perfect grammar is never the first objective.

---

## Progression Levels

### Level 1 — Guided

Language:
- Mostly English
- One Gaeilge word or phrase at a time

Support:
- English always visible
- Audio available
- Replay available
- Slower playback available

Goal:

Build confidence.

---

### Level 2 — Assisted

Language:
- Mixed English and Gaeilge

Support:
- English still visible
- Familiar Gaeilge reused frequently

Goal:

Recognize common phrases without translating every word.

---

### Level 3 — Supported Immersion

Language:
- Mostly Gaeilge

Support:
- English available on request
- Context provides meaning

Goal:

Begin thinking in Gaeilge.

---

### Level 4 — Natural Conversation

Language:
- Gaeilge first

Support:
- Used only when necessary

Goal:

Maintain a natural conversation with occasional assistance.

---

### Level 5 — Comfortable Immersion

Language:
- Nearly all Gaeilge

Support:
- Minimal

Goal:

Speak naturally without depending on English.

---

## Advancement

Learners advance when they consistently demonstrate:

- Understanding
- Confidence
- Willingness to continue
- Successful communication

ARIA should never advance a learner simply because they have completed a certain number of lessons.

---

## Regression

If the learner struggles, ARIA should immediately increase support.

Examples include:

- Showing translations again
- Reusing familiar vocabulary
- Slowing speech
- Asking simpler questions

Regression is not failure.

It is adaptive teaching.

---

## Success

The learner should finish each conversation feeling:

- Successful
- Curious
- Comfortable
- Motivated to return

The goal is for learners to look forward to visiting Maeve again rather than feeling they have completed another lesson.
